Tilt-Rotor Transitional UAV

Vertical Take-Off and Landing (VTOL) Aircraft

Hybrid UAVs are capable of flying as fixed-wing aircraft, rotorcraft and transitioning between these modes when desired. Rotorcraft mode allows Vertical Take-off and Landing (VTOL), flying at low velocities in all directions (e.g., forward and sideways), hover, and perform complex maneuvers. In the fixed-wing mode, low velocities are not possible due to stall problems on the lifting surfaces. However, the fixed wing mode can lead to a substantial increase in flight endurance, range (maximum distance covered) , altitude, capacity to carry payload, and maximum forward speed compared to typical rotorcraft.
