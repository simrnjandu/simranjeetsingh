Simulation of Impact of Newtonian Liquid Drop on Heated Plate
- In Progress

Abstract

This project focuses on the simulation of the impact of a Newtonian liquid drop on a heated plate using OpenFOAM, a widely used computational fluid dynamics (CFD) software. The study involves multiphase non-isothermal simulations of immiscible fluids with heat and mass transfer phenomena. The objective is to investigate the dynamic behavior and thermal characteristics of the liquid drop during impact on the heated surface.

The simulation setup considers a two-phase system consisting of a liquid drop and a surrounding gas phase, which comprises air and the vapor of the fluid. The numerical model incorporates the Navier-Stokes equations, energy equation, and mass transfer equations using the Lee mass transfer model to capture the complex interfacial dynamics, heat transfer, and vaporization phenomena.

By performing these simulations, we aim to gain insights into the intricate physics governing the impact process and understand the heat and mass transfer mechanisms associated with the system. The results will provide valuable information on the dynamics of liquid drop spreading, evaporation, vapor-phase behavior, and heat transfer, which can have significant implications in various real-life applications.


The application of such simulation studies in real-life problems includes, but is not limited to:

  1. Industrial Processes: Understanding the behavior of liquid droplets impacting heated surfaces is crucial in industries such as combustion systems, spray cooling, and chemical reactors. This simulation study can aid in optimizing process efficiency, improving heat transfer rates, and minimizing energy consumption.
  2. Thermal Management: In electronics cooling, understanding the behavior of liquid droplets on heated surfaces can help design more efficient cooling systems. By analyzing the spreading and evaporation characteristics, vapor-phase behavior, and heat transfer, this simulation study can provide insights into the development of enhanced cooling techniques for electronic devices.
  3. Fuel Injection and Combustion: The impact and atomization of liquid fuel droplets on hot surfaces play a vital role in fuel injection systems and combustion processes. This simulation study can assist in optimizing fuel spray characteristics, improving combustion efficiency, and reducing pollutant emissions.
  4. Environmental Science: Studying the impact of liquid droplets on heated surfaces can be relevant to environmental processes such as raindrop impacts on hot soil or the behavior of droplets during forest fires. This simulation study can contribute to a better understanding of natural phenomena and aid in the development of predictive models for environmental applications.

Overall, this project's simulation study provides valuable insights into the dynamics and heat transfer phenomena of liquid droplet impact on heated surfaces, enabling advancements in various industrial processes, thermal management techniques, fuel combustion optimization, and environmental studies.
