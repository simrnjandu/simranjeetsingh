A spiral heat exchanger has two or more metal plates rolled together into a spiral with some gap between the plates. The gap is maintained by having protrusions on the metal plates. The spiral of two or more plates with a gap between them creates two or more spiral channels that allow the flow of two different fluids in the heat exchanger. Due to this spiral configuration, a greater surface area can exchange heat between fluids in very compact and small spaces. This leads to good overall thermal conductivity of the heat exchanger.
